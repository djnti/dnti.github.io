About

I am a blind college student at SUNY Brockport. My degree is in Adapted Physical Education. I developed my own program for blind females about women in sport issues based off of my own experiences. I work as a one on one coach at Camp Abilities, teaching students how to participate in sports with a visual impairment. I participated in my first Ironman triathlon in the summer of 2022 with my guide John Slyer. I am believed to be the first blind athlete to have completed Ironman Lake Placid.

College student at SUNY Brockport & Adapted Physical Education Major.

Night had fallen and Megan Hale, a 21-year-old blind triathlete, had been swimming, cycling and running through the arduous mountain terrain of the Adirondacks for more than 16 hours straight. Physically exhausted and emotionally spent, she had a meltdown on the final stretch in the grueling long-distance punishment 2.4-mile swim, 112-mile bike and 26.2-mile run known as Ironman Lake Placid.
